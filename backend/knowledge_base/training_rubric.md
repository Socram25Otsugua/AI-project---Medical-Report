# Evaluation rubric (example)

## Completeness score (0–100)

- 0-30: insufficient information (missing vitals/ABCDE/problem)
- 31-60: partial (ABCDE present but missing measures/times/values)
- 61-85: good (almost complete; few missing details)
- 86-100: excellent (complete, clear, includes timeline and actions)

## Red flags (escalation)

- Airway not secured / inadequate ventilation
- Persistently low SpO2 despite O2
- Very low BP / very slow perfusion / cold and clammy skin
- Marked alteration in consciousness, convulsions
- Suspected spinal trauma
- Significant hypo/hyperthermia

## Text quality

- Numeric values with units
- Timeline (times)
- What was done and patient response
